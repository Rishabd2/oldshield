Analyzing public attitudes, news framing, and diffusion networks related to SHIELD COVID-19 testing through social media discourse and news coverage to understand its impact and inform best practices for future crises

 

Background information

SHIELD testing occurred in all regions of the state, with a concentration in the Chicagoland area (Figure 1). Zip codes outside of the Chicagoland area had somewhat lower total tests, ranging from a few hundred to a few thousands. In the Chicagoland area, the greatest concentration of a high number of tests was in the northern suburbs, such as Highland Park, Northbrook, Wheeling, and Des Plaines. The highest number of tests occurred in zip code 61761, with over 500,000 total tests. This data point was excluded because it is an outlier. The second-highest number of tests was in zip code 60540, which comprises most of Naperville, Illinois.

Elementary schools made up most of the SHIELD testing in Illinois with about 2.5 million tests, followed by middle schools then colleges and universities, each conducting about 1 million tests each. SHIELD testing primary targeted schools and universities in Illinois which is reflecting in the testing data. The largest non-school category was businesses, conducting about 500,000 tests total. Rivian, the electric car company based in the city of Normal, made up over 400,000 of these tests. Some government agencies included the governor's offices, court systems, and Argonne National Laboratory. In the healthcare providers category, various medical offices, hospitals, and rehabilitation centers. The "Other" category is made up of pop-up SHIELD testing sites, vocational schools, community testing sites and family and human services buildings.

 

Figure 5: SHIELD tests by agency type.

 

Most of the media coverage of SHIELD Illinois was in local state news, including the Chicago suburbs, according to Figure 6. This coverage was mostly in newspapers and their online editions but also included some TV stations and public media organizations. The second-largest type of newsrooms covering SHIELD were related to the University of Illinois system and their marketing teams. Most were stories on university websites that published stories about the successes of the SHIELD program. The Illinois Department of Public Health and other government agencies wrote various press releases and informational materials on SHIELD testing. Some notable national outlets that covered SHIELD include Politico, The New York Times, NPR, and The Wall Street Journal.

Of the 740 articles and content mentioning SHIELD Illinois, the large majority of them were news articles. A few trade publications, those aimed at industry professionals, discussed SHIELD and vaccination efficiency.

 

 

Figure 6: News coverage of SHIELD Illinois.

 

According to Figure 7, The highest number of tests coincided with the highest number of mentions on Twitter during the week of Jan. 16, 2022. There doesn't appear to be persistent patterns among the different measures of testing, Twitter activity, and media coverage. Except for news stories, where increases and decreases did align with testing changes from September 2021 to the end of May 2022. The most news stories were published during the first week of August 2021, presumably right before the start of the academic school year when a lot of school districts began implementing SHIELD testing.

Source-target dyad reciprocity

The highest count doesn't always mean the highest reciprocity score

Count: how many times two nodes are connected by an edge, regardless of the direction of the edge.

Reciprocity score: proportion of times that the edge between the two nodes is reciprocated, or the proportion of times that a directed edge goes in both directions. It is a measure of the symmetry or balance in the relationship between the two nodes.

 

 

We also visualized the frequency of twitter users mentioning each other. In previous edge lists, we removed rows if @SHIELDIlinois is mentioned. @JimFisher5105 is actively posting and mentioning SHIELD. @GovPritzker is often mentioned.

 

Our network analysis shows that:

        Between 2021-03-19 15:05:47 and 2022-03-11 11:04:36 (periods 1 and 2), @SHIELDIllinois mentioned a large number of accounts (n=60). In the last period, @SHIELDIllinois only mentioned 8 more accounts.

        Between 2021-08-14 19:55:50 and 2022-03-14 17:20:45 (period 2), we noticed a salient increase in the frequency of @SHIELDIlllinois being mentioned in others posts (an increase of 284)

        The account (@_chris_brooke) that was mentioned most in the official tweets of @SHIELDIlllinois is not the same account (@JimFisher5105) that mentioned @SHIELDIlllinois most in their posts - imbalance.

        There are however some accounts that were mentioned in SHIELDs tweets and also mentioned SHIELD in their posts: {'_chris_brooke', 'UofIllinois', 'rlsdvm_epivet'}

        Based on the Source-target dyad reciprocity score, it shows that SHIELD account does not engage in many meaningful interactions with the audience, particularly those KOLs.
